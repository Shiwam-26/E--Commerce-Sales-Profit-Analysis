# 🛍️ E-Commerce Sales & Profit Analysis

## Project Overview
In the competitive e-commerce market, understanding sales performance and profitability across products, regions, and customer segments is essential for growth. This project performs an end-to-end business analytics study on an e-commerce dataset using **Python**, uncovering what drives revenue and where profit is being lost.

---

## 🎯 Business Problem
**Question:** Which products, regions, and customer segments are most profitable — and where is the business losing money?

E-commerce companies deal with thousands of transactions daily. Without proper analysis, discount strategies can kill margins and certain segments go unnoticed. This project answers critical business questions using data.

## 📌 Steps Followed
1. **Data Loading & Exploration** — understood dataset shape, columns, and data types
2. **Data Cleaning** — handled null values, fixed data types, removed duplicates
3. **Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)** — univariate and bivariate analysis
4. **Sales Analysis** — revenue trends over time, top-performing categories
5. **Profit Analysis** — profit margins by category, region, and customer segment
6. **Customer Segment Analysis** — identified high-value vs low-value segments
7. **Trend Analysis** — monthly and seasonal sales patterns
8. **Business Insights & Recommendations** — actionable findings from the data

---

## 📊 Key Findings
- **Technology** category generates the highest revenue but not always the highest profit margin
- **Heavy discounting** on certain subcategories directly reduces profitability
- **Corporate and Consumer segments** drive the majority of sales volume
- Specific **regions underperform** consistently — indicating logistics or pricing issues
- **Q4 (Oct–Dec)** shows the highest sales spike due to seasonal demand
- Some **subcategories consistently run at a loss** despite high sales volume

---

## 💡 Business Recommendations
- Reduce or cap discounts on low-margin subcategories to protect profitability
- Prioritise marketing spend on top-performing regions and segments
- Investigate underperforming regions for pricing or delivery cost issues
- Stock up high-demand products ahead of Q4 to maximise seasonal revenue
- Focus upselling strategies on Corporate segment for higher average order value
